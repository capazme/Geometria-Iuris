## 1. KELSEN, Hans — *Reine Rechtslehre* (1960, 2nd ed.)

**Bibliographic reference**: KELSEN, H., *Reine Rechtslehre*, 2. Aufl., Franz Deuticke, Wien 1960. Studienausgabe ed. by M. Jestaedt, Mohr Siebeck, Tubingen 2017. English translation: *Pure Theory of Law*, transl. M. Knight, University of California Press, Berkeley 1967.

**Relevance to section 1.1**: Kelsen represents the most rigorous attempt to ground legal science on a purely normative, non-empirical foundation. His strict separation of Sein and Sollen and the Grundnorm as transcendental-logical presupposition exemplify the epistemological posture that section 1.1 problematizes: legal knowledge conceived as entirely autonomous from empirical measurement.

### Quote 1 — The Kant analogy [VERIFIED]
- Original: "So wie Kant fragt: wie ist eine von aller Metaphysik freie Deutung der unseren Sinnen gegebenen Tatsachen in den von der Naturwissenschaft formulierten Naturgesetzen moglich, so fragt die Reine Rechtslehre: wie ist eine nicht auf meta-rechtliche Autoritaten wie Gott oder Natur zuruckgreifende Deutung des subjektiven Sinns gewisser Tatbestande als ein System in Rechtssatzen beschreibbarer objektiv gultiger Rechtsnormen moglich?"
- Translation: "Just as Kant asks: how is a metaphysics-free interpretation of the facts given to our senses in the natural laws formulated by natural science possible, so the Pure Theory of Law asks: how is an interpretation of the subjective meaning of certain facts as a system of objectively valid legal norms describable in legal propositions possible, without recourse to meta-legal authorities such as God or Nature?"
- Location: *Reine Rechtslehre* (1960), p. 204-205; Knight transl. p. 202.
- Source of verification: The German passage is quoted in multiple secondary sources, including the DigiVolksgeist paper (Puzio, footnote 36, citing p. 361 of the Jestaedt Studienausgabe 2017) and confirmed by academic dissertations citing the 1960 Deuticke edition at pp. 204-205. The page discrepancy is due to different editions: p. 204-205 in the 1960 Deuticke original, p. 361 in the 2017 Jestaedt Studienausgabe.

### Quote 2 — Grundnorm as transcendental-logical presupposition [VERIFIED]
- Original: "[K]ann die Grundnorm [...] -- wenn ein Begriff der Kant'schen Erkenntnistheorie per analogiam angewendet werden darf -- als die transzendental-logische Bedingung dieser Deutung bezeichnet werden."
- Translation: "[T]he basic norm [...] -- if a concept from Kantian epistemology may be applied by analogy -- can be characterized as the transcendental-logical condition of this interpretation."
- Location: *Reine Rechtslehre* (1960), p. 204-205; Jestaedt Studienausgabe p. 361; Knight transl. p. 202.
- Source of verification: Quoted in DigiVolksgeist paper (Puzio, footnote 37) and confirmed by Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry on Pure Theory of Law [PT2, 202].

### Quote 3 — Sein/Sollen separation [VERIFIED]
- Original: "[A]us dem Umstand, dass etwas ist, [kann] nicht folgen [...], dass etwas sein soll, so wie daraus, dass etwas sein soll, nicht folgen kann, dass etwas ist."
- Translation: "From the fact that something is, it cannot follow that something ought to be, just as from the fact that something ought to be, it cannot follow that something is."
- Location: *Reine Rechtslehre* (1960), pp. 5-6 (opening chapter: "Recht und Natur").
- Source of verification: Widely cited in secondary literature (Wikipedia "Reine Rechtslehre"; JuraForum; Kelsen-Institut). The Sein/Sollen dualism is presented on pp. 5ff., 16, 19, 196, 215ff. of the 1960 edition.

## 2. GADAMER, Hans-Georg — *Wahrheit und Methode* (1960)

**Bibliographic reference**: GADAMER, H.-G., *Wahrheit und Methode. Grundzuge einer philosophischen Hermeneutik*, J.C.B. Mohr (Paul Siebeck), Tubingen 1960, pp. xvii + 486. Later reprinted in *Gesammelte Werke*, Bd. 1, Mohr Siebeck, Tubingen 1990. English translation: *Truth and Method*, 2nd rev. ed., transl. J. Weinsheimer and D.G. Marshall, Continuum, New York 2004.

**Relevance to section 1.1**: Gadamer represents the hermeneutic tradition's explicit rejection of the applicability of natural-science methods to the humanities. His argument that understanding is not a technique applied to an object but a mode of participation in tradition provides the philosophical foundation for the claim that legal science has historically resisted measurement.

### Quote 1 — Understanding as participation, not method [UNVERIFIED]
- Original: "Verstehen ist nicht so sehr eine Methode, durch die sich der erkennende Geist an ein ihm fremdes Objekt wendet [...] als vielmehr ein dem Sein des Verstandenen selbst Zugehoriges."
- Translation: "Understanding is not so much a method through which the knowing mind approaches an alien object [...] but rather something that belongs to the very being of what is understood."
- Location: Approximate: Part II, ch. 1, around pp. 270-280 of the 1960 edition.
- Source of verification: The concept is confirmed across all secondary literature (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, Wikipedia "Wahrheit und Methode"). However, the exact German wording and page number could not be verified via web sources. The Weinsheimer/Marshall translation and the Gadamer entry on SEP confirm the concept but no source reproduced this exact phrasing with a page reference.

### Quote 2 — Wirkungsgeschichtliches Bewusstsein [VERIFIED]
- Original: "Das wirkungsgeschichtliche Bewusstsein ist [...] in erster Linie Bewusstsein der hermeneutischen Situation."
- Translation: "The consciousness of being affected by history (historically effected consciousness) is [...] primarily awareness of the hermeneutical situation."
- Location: Part II, ch. 2 ("The Hermeneutic Significance of Temporal Distance"), around pp. 284-290 of the 1960 edition.
- Source of verification: Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, entry on Gadamer (SEP); English translation confirmed in Weinsheimer/Marshall edition, Part II.

### Quote 3 — Rehabilitation of prejudice [VERIFIED]
- Original: "Die Uberwindung aller Vorurteile, diese Pauschalforderung der Aufklarung, wird sich selber als ein Vorurteil erweisen."
- Translation: "The overcoming of all prejudices, this global demand of the Enlightenment, will itself prove to be a prejudice."
- Location: Part II, ch. 1 ("The hermeneutic circle and the problem of prejudice"), around pp. 254-261 of the 1960 edition.
- Source of verification: Quoted and confirmed in Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (Gadamer entry) and multiple GRIN academic papers.

### Quote 4 — The anticipation of meaning and the hermeneutic circle [VERIFIED]
- Concept: The anticipation of meaning (Sinnerwartung / Vorgriff der Vollkommenheit) that governs understanding is not an act of subjectivity but proceeds from the commonality that binds us to tradition.
- Translation (Weinsheimer/Marshall): "The anticipation of meaning that governs our understanding of a text is not an act of subjectivity, but proceeds from the commonality that binds us to the tradition."
- Location: Part II, Section on "The hermeneutic significance of temporal distance."
- Source of verification: Confirmed in Weinsheimer/Marshall translation, cited in MIT reading packet (web.mit.edu/kaclark/www/gadamer_truth_and_method.pdf) and multiple secondary sources.

## 3. BETTI, Emilio — *Teoria generale della interpretazione* (1955)

**Bibliographic reference**: BETTI, E., *Teoria generale della interpretazione*, 2 voll., A. Giuffre, Milano 1955 (Pubblicazioni dell'Istituto di Teoria della Interpretazione presso le Universita di Roma e di Camerino). German abridged version: *Allgemeine Auslegungslehre als Methodik der Geisteswissenschaften*, Mohr Siebeck, Tubingen 1967. Corrected edition ed. by G. Crifo, Giuffre, Milano 1990.

**Relevance to section 1.1**: Betti's hermeneutic theory provides the most systematic attempt to reconcile objectivity in interpretation with the humanities' distinctive methods. His four canons of interpretation represent legal hermeneutics' self-conscious methodological framework -- one that explicitly rejects quantitative measurement while claiming scientific rigor.

### Quote 1 — Four canons of interpretation [VERIFIED]
- The four canons, divided ex parte objecti and ex parte subjecti:
  1. **Canone dell'autonomia ermeneutica** (canon of hermeneutic autonomy): representative forms must be understood according to the spirit objectified in them ("secondo quello spirito che si e obiettivato in esse"), not according to the interpreter's projections.
  2. **Canone della totalita** (canon of totality and coherence): one must always keep present the correlation "tra le parti [...] e il tutto di cui fanno parte o a cui si ricollegano" ("between the parts and the whole of which they are part or to which they are linked").
  3. **Canone dell'attualita dell'intendere** (canon of the actuality of understanding): the interpreter necessarily brings a present perspective, but must not distort the object into anachronism.
  4. **Canone dell'adeguazione dell'intendere** (canon of the adequacy of understanding): the interpreter must be (or become) congenial to the object, adopting the most favorable perspective.
- Location: Vol. I, pp. 305-367.
- Source of verification: Treccani Enciclopedia (entry "Betti, Emilio"), confirmed by Redalyc (Hermeneutica objetiva: el caso de Emilio Betti), Wikipedia (Emilio Betti entry), and SPEP papers.

### Quote 2 — Interpretation as recognition of objectified meaning [VERIFIED]
- Concept: Interpretation is the process of recognizing meaning that has been objectified in representative forms (*forme rappresentative*). It is not subjective attribution (*Sinngebung*) but objective recognition (*riconoscimento di senso*). Betti explicitly polemicizes against Gadamer's supposed subjectivism.
- Location: Vol. I, Premessa (Preface) and ch. 1-3.
- Source of verification: Treccani Enciclopedia confirms: Betti denounced *Sinngebung* as "il pericolo maggiore dell'ermeneutica contemporanea" and opposed it with objective hermeneutics grounded in the autonomy of the interpreted object.

### Quote 3 — Hermeneutics as general methodology of the Geisteswissenschaften [VERIFIED]
- Concept: Betti conceived his theory as "la piu ambiziosa e articolata ermeneutica intesa come metodica generale delle scienze dello spirito del XX secolo" ("the most ambitious and articulated hermeneutics understood as a general methodology of the sciences of the spirit of the 20th century").
- Location: General characterization from secondary literature.
- Source of verification: Treccani Enciclopedia, Arbor Sapientiae catalog, TAB Edizioni description.

## 4. BOBBIO, Norberto — *Teoria della scienza giuridica* (1950)

**Bibliographic reference**: BOBBIO, N., *Teoria della scienza giuridica*, Giappichelli, Torino 1950. The key essay was later republished as "Scienza del diritto e analisi del linguaggio" in *Rivista trimestrale di diritto e procedura civile*, 1950, and reprinted in: BOBBIO, N., *Saggi sulla scienza giuridica*, Giappichelli, Torino 2011, ISBN 978-88-348-2055-1.

**Relevance to section 1.1**: Bobbio's characterization of legal science as a meta-linguistic discipline -- a discourse upon the prescriptive discourse of the legislator -- illustrates the epistemological self-understanding of 20th-century legal positivism. Legal science, for Bobbio, is inherently descriptive of prescriptions, not productive of empirical knowledge. This frames the "gap" that section 1.1 identifies.

### Quote 1 — Legal science as meta-language [VERIFIED]
- Concept: "Il diritto e il discorso del legislatore e la scienza giuridica non e altro che un discorso sul discorso: un metalinguaggio descrittivo del linguaggio prescrittivo del legislatore."
- Translation: "Law is the discourse of the legislator and legal science is nothing but a discourse upon a discourse: a meta-language descriptive of the prescriptive language of the legislator."
- Location: "Scienza del diritto e analisi del linguaggio" (1950), passim.
- Source of verification: Confirmed in multiple secondary sources: Palermo IRIS repository ("La scienza del diritto e (solo) analisi del linguaggio?"), Academia.edu paper (same title), Bovero's tribute in *Ratio Juris* (2005). The formulation is widely attributed to Bobbio in Italian legal philosophy.

### Quote 2 — Legal science as prescriptive metascience [UNVERIFIED]
- Concept: Bobbio argued that legal science occupies a peculiar epistemological position: it is a science whose object is itself prescriptive. It is therefore a "metascienza prescrittiva" -- not a science that prescribes, but a science *about* prescriptions. This makes it fundamentally different from natural sciences, which describe empirical regularities.
- Location: *Teoria della scienza giuridica* (1950), chapters I-II.
- Source of verification: The concept is confirmed across secondary literature, but the exact phrase "metascienza prescrittiva" could not be located verbatim in any web source. The concept is clear; the exact wording needs manual verification.

## 5. LARENZ, Karl — *Methodenlehre der Rechtswissenschaft* (1991, 6th ed.)

**Bibliographic reference**: LARENZ, K., *Methodenlehre der Rechtswissenschaft*, 6. Aufl., Springer-Verlag, Berlin-Heidelberg 1991, pp. XVIII + 495. ISBN 978-3-540-52872-2 (hardcover); ISBN 978-3-662-08711-4 (eBook).

**Relevance to section 1.1**: Larenz's systematic presentation of legal methodology -- grounded in the tradition from Savigny through the hermeneutic school -- represents the canonical self-understanding of German Rechtswissenschaft. His account of Savigny's four canons of interpretation demonstrates how legal science has conceived its method: as interpretative, not quantitative; as hermeneutic, not experimental.

### Quote 1 — Savigny's four canons of interpretation [VERIFIED]
- Concept: Following Savigny (*System des heutigen romischen Rechts*, Bd. I, Veit, Berlin 1840, pp. 213-214), Larenz presents four "Elemente" (elements/canons) of interpretation:
  1. **Das grammatische Element** — the meaning of words and their linguistic context
  2. **Das logische Element** — the logical structure of the proposition and its internal coherence
  3. **Das historische Element** — the historical circumstances that gave rise to the norm
  4. **Das systematische Element** — the relationship of the norm to the broader legal system
- Location: Larenz, *Methodenlehre* (1991), Part I (historical-critical section), chapter on Savigny. Savigny's original formulation: *System*, Bd. I, pp. 213-214 (section 33).
- Source of verification: Confirmed in klartext-jura.de ("Die klassischen canones nach Savigny"), GRIN academic papers, Wikipedia "Auslegung (Recht)", and Springer Nature chapter listing for the book.

### Note on Larenz's reception
Larenz's work has been controversial due to his involvement with National Socialist legal theory. For section 1.1, the relevant point is methodological: the *Methodenlehre* codifies the hermeneutic self-understanding of German legal science, regardless of its author's political history. The 6th edition (1991) is the standard post-war reference.

## 6. ENGISCH, Karl — *Einfuhrung in das juristische Denken* (1956/1983)

**Bibliographic reference**: ENGISCH, K., *Einfuhrung in das juristische Denken*, W. Kohlhammer, Stuttgart 1956 (1st ed.); 8. Aufl. 1983. Currently available: 13. Aufl., ed. by T. Wurtenberger and D. Otto, Kohlhammer, Stuttgart 2024, ISBN 978-3-17-044919-0. The 8th edition (1983) is the standard scholarly reference.

**Relevance to section 1.1**: Engisch's presentation of subsumption (*Subsumtion*) as the basic logical operation of legal reasoning illustrates the dominant model of legal method: deductive application of general norms to particular cases. This model is inherently non-quantitative -- it operates through conceptual classification, not measurement. Section 1.1 can use Engisch to show what legal "science" has traditionally meant.

### Quote 1 — Subsumption as classification [VERIFIED]
- Original: "Die Subsumtion eines konkreten Sachverhaltes unter einen Begriff kann nur als die Einordnung des Sachverhaltes in die Klasse der von der Rechtsregel bezeichneten Falle verstanden werden."
- Translation: "The subsumption of a concrete factual situation under a concept can only be understood as the classification of the factual situation into the class of cases designated by the legal rule."
- Location: Chapter III ("Die Gewinnung konkreter Rechtsurteile aus der Rechtsnorm, insbesondere das Problem der Subsumtion"), approximately pp. 53-59 of the 8th edition (1983); pp. 104-105 of the 2010 edition.
- Source of verification: Wikipedia "Subsumtion (Recht)", RechtEasy.at, Kohlhammer eLibrary (table of contents confirms Chapter III topic). The exact German quote is reproduced in RechtEasy.at and Studocu secondary sources.

### Quote 2 — The syllogistic structure [VERIFIED]
- Concept: Legal reasoning proceeds as a syllogism:
  - Major premise: the legal norm (Rechtsnorm)
  - Minor premise: the factual situation (Sachverhalt)
  - Conclusion: the legal judgment (Rechtsfolge)
- The process of subsumption is the operation by which the minor premise is brought under the major premise.
- Location: Chapter III.
- Source of verification: Kohlhammer eLibrary (chapter description), Wikipedia "Subsumtion (Recht)", Gabler Wirtschaftslexikon.
